You may need to install packages! If you are somehow struggling google it. The bare minimum you can do is this. Please do more. ``` #Getting stuff #Load library library("DESeq2") #if not installed, google it or bioconductor. #chose the tsv file path datafile=file.choose() #make it a table instance cds = read.table( datafile, header=TRUE, row.names=1) #tell it what stuff is. Use your conditions obviously. des=data.frame(row.names=colnames( cds),condition=c("UM5","UM5","UM1","UM1","UR5","UR5","UR1","UR1","AM5","AM5","AM1","AM1"),aerobicity=c("unaero","unaero","unaero","unaero","unaero","unaero","unaero","unaero","aero","aero","aero","aero"),richness=c("min","min","min","min","rich","rich","rich","rich","min","min","min","min"),speed=c("0.05/h","0.05/h","0.1/h","0.1/h","0.05/h","0.05/h","0.1/h","0.1/h","0.05/h","0.05/h","0.1/h","0.1/h")) #Normalise the data for general comparisons. dds <- DESeqDataSetFromMatrix(countData = cds, colData = des,design = ~ aerobicity) #rld is regularised log2 of counts. rld <- rlog(dds) #save write.csv(as.data.frame(assay(rld)),file="F:\\RNASeq\\R analysis\\1_rld.csv") #Do analysis #Choose the columns you want. if you want all just use cds and des and not cds[,1:8] RvM <- DESeqDataSetFromMatrix(countData = cds[,1:8], colData = des[1:8,],design = ~ richness) #Delete the conditions you don't. RvM$aerobicity <- droplevels(RvM$aerobicity) #Do this if you have multiple conditions to compare. design(RvM) <-formula(~ richness + speed) #Do the analysis RvM <-DESeq(RvM) resR <-results(RvM, contrast =c("richness","rich","min")) write.csv(as.data.frame(resR),file="F:\\RNASeq\\R analysis\\1_rich.csv") ``` So in the csv you will have: * Log2 fold change between of the contrast you asked for: `contrast =c("richness","rich","min")` means you are comparing along the richness condition axis, the FC will be the change from min to rich. * Log2 SE. * negative binomial GLM fitted p-values and BH-adjusted p-values. If you need to do operations with the log2s remember they are logarithms, so don't do geometric means, just regular averages (arithmetic mean) ##Analysis in Excel There are loads of things you can do. ###Biggest changers One of these is sorting by biggest changers. Footnote. Official description of Geobacillus thermoglucosidans/thermoglucosidans, description paper says:

Emended description of Geobacillus thermoglucosidans nom. corrig.

Geobacillus thermoglucosidans (ther.mo.glu.co′si.dans Gr. n. thermê heat; N.L. part. adj. glucosidans glucosidating, i.e. releasing glucoside units; N.L. part. adj. thermoglucosidans indicating the production of heat-stable glucosidase).

Basonym: Bacillus thermoglucosidasius Suzuki et al. 1983.

Gram-positive, motile rods 0.5–1.2×3.0–7.0 µm. Short ellipsoidal to cylindrical endospores are borne subterminally and terminally and occasionally swell the sporangia. Facultatively anaerobic. Colonies on TSA are 0.5 to 5 mm in diameter, flat, smooth, translucent, glossy, circular and entire, and faintly brown in colour after 50 °C incubation for 24–48 h. Viscid pellicles are formed in broth. Optimum growth at 50 °C; no growth at 37 or 60 °C. Optimum growth at pH 8, weak growth at pH 7 and 9, and no growth at pH 6. Tolerates up to 0.5 % NaCl. Catalase-positive, oxidase weakly positive. Aesculin is hydrolysed, casein is not. Starch hydrolysis is variable and weak when positive. Citrate is not utilized; arginine dihydrolase, hydrogen sulphide, indole, lysine decarboxylase, tryptophan deaminase and urease production are negative; and the Voges–Proskauer test and nitrate reduction are variable. Acid without gas is produced from cellobiose, d-glucose, d-fructose, maltose, mannitol, d-mannose, methyl d-glucoside, N-acetylglucosamine, ribose, salicin, sucrose, trehalose, turanose and d-xylose. Acid is produced weakly from glycerol and lactose. Acid production is variable from amygdalin, l-arabinose, arbutin, gentiobiose, rhamnose, sorbitol, l-sorbose (weak when positive) and starch. No acid is produced from adonitol, d-arabinose, d- and l-arabitol, dulcitol, erythritol, d- and l-fucose, galactose, gluconate, glycogen, inulin, 2- and 5-keto- d-gluconate, d-lyxose, melezitose, melibiose, myo-inositol, methyl d-mannoside, methyl xyloside, raffinose, d-tagatose, xylitol or l-xylose. In the variable reactions listed above, the type strain does not produce cylindrical spores or swollen sporangia; it hydrolyses starch weakly; is negative for nitrate reduction and the Voges–Proskauer test, and produces acid from l-arabinose, amygdalin, arbutin, gentiobiose, rhamnose, sorbitol, l-sorbose (weakly) and starch. Large amounts of exo-oligo-1,6-glucosidase are synthesized ( Suzuki et al., 1983 ). The main cellular fatty acids are iso-C 15 : 0, iso-C 16 : 0, C 16 : 0, iso-C 17 : 0 and anteiso-C 17 : 0, which make up 90 % of the total. The DNA G+C content is 43.1–43.4 mol% (HPLC).

The type strain is LMG 7137 T ( = ATCC 43742 T = DSM 2542 T = NCIMB 11955 T = R-35637 T), isolated from soil. The DNA G+C content of the type strain is 43.4 mol% (by HPLC).
